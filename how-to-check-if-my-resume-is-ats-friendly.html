Are you applying for jobs online and are waiting for a response? Your resume might need to be ATS-friendly. Many companies use an Applicant Tracking System (ATS) to screen resumes and narrow the candidate pool. This means your resume needs to be optimized for ATS compatibility to increase your chances of getting hired. In this guide, we will go over some tips on how to check if your resume is ATS-friendly and how to make the necessary adjustments.

To determine if your resume is ATS (Applicant Tracking System) friendly, compare it to an ATS-optimized example. Pay attention to the following aspects:

  1. Chronological format
  2. Relevant keywords
  3. Clear headings
  4. Degrees and abbreviations
  5. Career target

What is Applicant Tracking System (ATS) and how does it work?

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is a software tool many companies and recruitment agencies use to manage their recruitment process. ATS helps employers streamline and automate hiring by screening and sorting job applications based on specific criteria. This saves hiring managers significant time and effort, especially when they receive many applications.

ATS scans resumes and job applications for keywords and phrases related to the job description and required qualifications. It then ranks and filters the applications based on the relevance of the keywords and phrases found in each resume. If your resume has the necessary keywords, it may pass the ATS screening process and never be seen by the hiring manager. So, why does ATS matter for job seekers?

With an estimated 75% of resumes being rejected by ATS before they even reach a human recruiter, ensuring that your resume is ATS-friendly is essential. How to Make an ATS-Compliant Resume?

ATS-Compatible Resume Formatting

It's crucial to use a standard, easy-to-read format for your resume to ensure it's ATS-friendly. Avoid using images, charts, tables, and other visual elements that may confuse an ATS. Stick to a traditional, reverse-chronological format with straightforward headings. Hiring managers prefer this format, and it's easy for ATS to process. On our website, you can find resume templates designed for ATS compatibility and tips on popular resume formats which also showcase about writing proper resume headlines and so on.

Keyword Optimization

Keyword Optimization for ATS One of the most critical aspects of an ATS-friendly resume is optimizing it with relevant keywords. Keywords refer to words that an ATS may search for in a job application that match the job description or industry. Your resume should already contain core industry keywords, but reviewing the job description for additional keywords to include is essential. A skills section with relevant keywords can help you quickly swap them in and out when applying for different positions.

Send Your Resume as a Word Document

Sending your resume as a Word document, specifically a .doc or .docx file, ensures that it can be quickly processed by all ATS systems and is preferred by most hiring managers. Although some ATS systems now process PDFs, some may still need help. It's not worth the risk of losing formatting, so sending your resume as a Word .doc is best to guarantee that it's ATS-friendly.

Spell Out Abbreviations

An ATS may not understand all abbreviations, so it's essential to spell them out at least once. Additionally, spelling out abbreviations helps recruiters and hiring managers who may need to become more familiar with industry-specific jargon. This ensures that everyone reviewing your resume can easily understand what you do. For example, spell "Certified Public Accountant" instead of "CPA."

Include Relevant Information

The ATS scans your resume to determine if it contains relevant information and experience for a particular job. Tailoring your resume to the specific job and industry you're applying for is crucial which can be the first step for the preparation of your job application process . Include only relevant experience and education, highlighting transferable skills if you have yet to gain direct experience. Only submit a resume that contains relevant information that may be clear to the ATS.

How to Test Your Resume for ATS-Compatibility?

Several online tools are available to test your resume's ATS compatibility. They are:

Jobscan

To use Jobscan, copy and paste your resume and the job description into the respective fields on the website. Jobscan will then analyze your resume and provide a score out of 100, along with feedback on how to improve your resume's ATS compatibility.

RezRunner

Another tool to consider is RezRunner, which uses artificial intelligence to scan your resume for keywords, formatting, and other factors that affect ATS compatibility. Once the scan is complete, you will receive a report with detailed feedback and suggestions on optimizing your resume for ATS.

When interpreting the results of an ATS test, pay attention to the feedback and suggestions provided. Look for areas where your resume could be improved, such as using more relevant keywords or adjusting formatting. Remember that optimizing your resume for ATS compatibility is just one aspect of a successful job search, and it's essential to tailor your resume and cover letter to each job you apply for.

ATS-friendly resume test 1: Save your resume in plain text

Saving a copy of your resume as plain text is a quick and easy way to check if your resume is ATS-friendly. Applicant tracking systems often need help to read embedded tables, symbols, charts, images, and other design elements. If the ATS software does not recognize these design elements correctly, it may skip over important information in those graphics or return them as gibberish.

When saving your resume as plain text, check for missing details, incorrect characters, and disorganized formatting. Correct any issues before submitting your resume to ensure the ATS software can adequately read and interpret it.
